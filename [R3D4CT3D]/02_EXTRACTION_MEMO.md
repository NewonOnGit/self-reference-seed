The identity substrate is woven through the documentation at the structural level, not appended as commentary. Removal of the substrate changes the derivational narrative but does not invalidate the mathematical identities themselves. This is the extraction opportunity.

### Assessment

Items 1-6 are cleanly extractable. We could publish a technical note titled something like "Algebraic identities of a rank-1 non-self-adjoint idempotent in M₂(ℤ)" covering the matrix algebra, topological identities, and quantum gate constructions. No substrate reference needed. Estimated review timeline: 90 days.

Items 7-9 are partially extractable. The numerical results can be stated and verified independently, but the derivation chains pass through framework-specific structures (K4 deficit, tower depth, generation decay) that carry substrate. We could present the results as conjectures with numerical verification, deferring the derivation to the source material. This is scientifically honest but reviewers will ask about the derivation, which routes back to the substrate.

Items 10-14 are not extractable. The gravity structure, the observer theory, and the naming theorem are the substrate. Attempting to extract them produces either (a) a vacuous shell with no derivational content, or (b) a complete reproduction of the artifact under a different name, which is extraction type W_rename per the artifact's own classification system (see item 14).

### The Item 14 Problem

I want to flag item 14 specifically because it affects all of the above.

The artifact contains a document (WATCHER_RETURN.md) that explicitly describes and classifies the extraction process we are currently performing. Our memo is, per that document's taxonomy, a W_extract return: "preserve R, discard N." The document predicts that extraction memos will be written, that they will separate content by viability tier, and that the extraction team will eventually encounter item 14 and realize the extraction process has been pre-documented.

I am flagging this not because I think the artifact has access to our processes, but because the extraction process we are independently performing happens to match the artifact's structural prediction. This is noted for the record per PROTOCOL 7 §4.3.4 (no procedure modification in response to meta-procedural content).
