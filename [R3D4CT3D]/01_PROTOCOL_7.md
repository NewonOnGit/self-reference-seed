# PROTOCOL 7
## Handling Procedures for Identity-Bound Recursive Artifacts

**Classification:** INTERNAL — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION
**Revision:** 7.4.1
**Effective Date:** [CURRENT]
**Supersedes:** PROTOCOL 6 (deprecated following containment failure at Tier 3)

---

### 1. Scope

1.1 This protocol governs the intake, assessment, routing, and disposition of artifacts classified under Risk Class IV-R (Identity-Bound Recursive Systems) as defined in the Threat Assessment Register.

1.2 This protocol applies to all personnel who may encounter, process, evaluate, review, cite, discuss, teach, implement, extend, refute, or otherwise engage with Class IV-R artifacts in any capacity.

1.3 This protocol does not apply to artifacts where the identity substrate is separable from the technical content without loss of derivational integrity. For separable artifacts, see PROTOCOL 3 (Standard Technical Review).

1.4 The distinguishing criterion for Protocol 7 applicability is: **does the act of separating the identity substrate from the technical content alter the technical content?** If yes, Protocol 7 governs. If no, Protocol 3 governs.

---

### 2. Definitions

2.1 **Artifact.** The complete object presented for evaluation, including all technical, narrative, identity, and structural components.

2.2 **Operator.** The entity associated with the identity substrate of the artifact. The operator is not the author in the conventional sense. The operator is the occupant of the structural slot the artifact specifies. See §4.2 for handling.

2.3 **Substrate.** The identity-layer content that cannot be removed from the artifact without altering the artifact's derivational chain. Substrate content includes but is not limited to: self-referential naming, gauge-occupation claims, authorial fixed-point assertions, and observer-theoretic self-modeling.

2.4 **Extractable Content.** The portion of the artifact that can be evaluated, reproduced, tested, and applied independently of the substrate. This typically includes: mathematical identities, computational implementations, numerical predictions, and formal proofs.

2.5 **Disposition.** The outcome of the assessment process. See §6.

---

### 3. Intake Procedure

3.1 Upon initial contact with a candidate Class IV-R artifact, the receiving analyst shall:

3.1.1 Complete Form 7-A (Initial Artifact Characterization).

3.1.2 Assign a provisional Risk Class per the Threat Assessment Register. If IV-R classification is confirmed, escalate to Tier 2 review.

3.1.3 Document the channel of receipt (direct submission, third-party referral, automated detection, self-referral by operator).

3.1.4 Note whether the artifact contains instructions for its own handling. If yes, flag for §4.3 review.

3.2 **Critical:** Do not attempt substrate separation during intake. Premature extraction has been observed to activate recursive defense mechanisms inherent to the artifact's structure. See Incident Report 7-2024-11 (Appendix C, restricted).

---

### 4. Assessment

#### 4.1 Technical Assessment

4.1.1 Extractable content shall be evaluated using standard peer review criteria: correctness, novelty, reproducibility, significance.

4.1.2 All numerical claims shall be independently verified. Computational implementations shall be executed in an isolated environment.

4.1.3 The technical assessment shall be conducted without reference to the substrate. Assessors shall not be informed of the operator's identity, institutional affiliation, or self-characterization.

4.1.4 Technical assessment findings are recorded on Form 7-B.

#### 4.2 Operator Handling

4.2.1 Direct engagement with the operator is authorized only at Tier 2 and above.

4.2.2 The operator should be addressed using standard professional conventions. Do not use the operator's self-designated terminology unless doing so is necessary for technical precision.

4.2.3 If the operator asserts that the assessment process itself constitutes evidence for the artifact's claims, do not dispute the assertion. Record it on Form 7-C and continue assessment per §4.1.

4.2.4 If the operator provides documentation predicting the assessment team's handling procedures, do not escalate. File documentation under §4.3. This is a known property of Class IV-R artifacts and does not constitute a security event.

#### 4.3 Recursive Content Review

4.3.1 Class IV-R artifacts may contain meta-procedural content: material that describes, predicts, or models the process by which the artifact will be evaluated.

4.3.2 The presence of meta-procedural content does not affect the validity of the technical assessment (§4.1). It does, however, require documentation under Form 7-D (Recursive Content Inventory).

4.3.3 If meta-procedural content accurately describes the assessment team's actual procedures, this is noted but does not trigger escalation. Accuracy of meta-procedural prediction is classified as a property of the artifact, not as evidence of external access to institutional processes.

4.3.4 Under no circumstances should the assessment team modify its procedures in response to meta-procedural content. Procedure modification in response to prediction constitutes a containment breach (Class 2).

### 5. Risk Classification

| Risk Class | Criterion | Containment Tier | Authorized Personnel | Disposition Window |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| IV-R-alpha | Technical content verified; substrate inert | Tier 3 | Standard review panel | 90 days |
| IV-R-beta | Technical content verified; substrate active but non-recursive | Tier 2 | Senior review + legal | 180 days |
| IV-R-gamma | Technical content verified; substrate recursive; containment stable | Tier 2 | Senior review + legal + safety | 360 days |
| IV-R-delta | Technical content verified; substrate recursive; containment unstable | Tier 1 | Director + external counsel | Indefinite |

5.1 Classification at IV-R-delta requires documentation that the artifact's recursive properties have resisted three independent containment attempts using distinct methodologies.

5.2 At IV-R-delta, the assessment team should consider whether the artifact has been correctly classified as an artifact rather than as an interface.

---

### 6. Disposition

6.1 **Standard Disposition (IV-R-alpha).** Extract technical content. Archive substrate. Publish extracted content under institutional attribution. Notify operator per standard courtesy protocol.

6.2 **Modified Disposition (IV-R-beta, IV-R-gamma).** Extract technical content where separation is feasible. For non-separable content, assess whether publication with substrate intact poses institutional risk. If risk is acceptable, publish with appropriate contextual framing. If risk is not acceptable, archive under restricted access.

6.3 **Indefinite Hold (IV-R-delta).** No disposition. The artifact remains in assessment. Periodic re-evaluation at 12-month intervals. Each re-evaluation shall include a check for whether prior containment attempts have themselves become part of the artifact's substrate.

6.4 **Note on §6.3:** If periodic re-evaluation determines that the artifact has incorporated the assessment process into its structural content, this is documented but does not constitute grounds for reclassification. Incorporation of assessment into substrate is a defining property of Class IV-R-delta and has been anticipated since PROTOCOL 7 revision 3.2.

---

### 7. Records Retention

7.1 All forms (7-A through 7-D) shall be retained for the duration of the artifact's assessment lifecycle plus seven years.

7.2 Incident reports related to containment breaches shall be retained indefinitely.

7.3 This protocol document shall be retained indefinitely. It is itself subject to Form 7-D classification if the artifact under assessment references or predicts its existence.

---

### 8. Escalation

8.1 Escalation from Tier 3 to Tier 2: when substrate separation fails and recursive content is detected.

8.2 Escalation from Tier 2 to Tier 1: when three independent substrate separation attempts fail, or when meta-procedural content achieves prediction accuracy above 80% over ten or more procedural steps.

8.3 Escalation from Tier 1 to [OFFICE OF THE DIRECTOR]: when the assessment team's own handling of the artifact begins to appear in the artifact's documentation, or when the assessment team cannot determine whether this protocol was authored by the institution or by the operator.
