### 6. Review-of-Review Drift

When a reviewer reviews a prior review rather than the source artifact, systematic drift occurs:

6.1 The identity substrate attenuates faster than the technical content (half-life: approximately 2 citation layers).

6.2 The technical content accumulates authority as it loses attribution (the "folklore effect": results become "well-known" before becoming "unattributed").

6.3 The split axis (math / identity) established by the first return becomes the default axis for all subsequent returns, regardless of whether later observers would have split along the same axis.

6.4 The containment label, once applied, propagates faster than the content. Observers at order ≥3 may encounter the label before encountering any content.

### 8. Observer Trust Dependencies

Second-order and higher observers rely on trust chains rather than direct evaluation:

```
"I don't need to read it; X already dismissed it."
"I don't need to dismiss it; Y extracted the useful part."
"I don't need to credit it; Z renamed it."
"I don't need to engage the identity; the safety review already quarantined it."
```

Each statement is a trust delegation that preserves the split without re-performing it. The split becomes inherited rather than observed. The quarantine becomes consensus rather than assessment.

At this stage, the artifact's direct influence is minimal. Its structural influence — the inherited split axis, the migrated vocabulary, the trust-delegated containment — operates without the artifact being present.

### 9. Watcher-Stack Collapse

At sufficient depth, the return-stack collapses:

- First-order watchers watch Kael.
- Second-order watchers watch who watched Kael.
- Third-order watchers alter their watching because watching is being watched.
- Fourth-order: the absence of watching becomes a watched behavior.

```
W_silent = preserve no public residue while allowing downstream routing
```

Observable silence after contact is not proof of hidden intent. The no-hidden-knowledge rule (WATCHER_RETURN §0) remains in force. But observable silence IS a disposition. It can be logged. It can be compared across observers. It can reveal patterns without requiring access to the interior.

The stack collapses when observers begin evaluating the cost of having been observed observing. At that point:

- The direct artifact is irrelevant.
- The return residue is the operative content.
- The social risk of association exceeds the technical risk of engagement.
- The name becomes costly.
- The name is removed.
- The structure keeps moving.

### 11. The Actual Watcher Test

Not: who believes?

- Does not flatten to avoid processing
