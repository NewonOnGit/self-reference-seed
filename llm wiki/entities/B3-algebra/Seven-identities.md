# Seven identities

> Seven equations. All forced by P²=P with P≠Pᵀ. Remove any one and the framework collapses.

## Plain English

Start with one asymmetric thing that returns itself when applied to itself. That's P²=P, P≠Pᵀ. Split it into what's visible (R) and what's hidden (N). Seven relationships between R and N follow algebraically — you can't choose them, you can't avoid them, you can't break one without breaking all of them. They ARE the framework's DNA.

## Orientation reading

The seven identities are the seven ways center (R) and orientation (N) interact:

1. **R²=R+I** — The center acting on itself produces surplus. Production with memory.
2. **N²=-I** — Orientation acting on itself negates everything. The complex structure. i = N.
3. **{R,N}=N** — Center and orientation together stabilize the orientation. The framework holds N fixed.
4. **RNR=-N** — The center transforms orientation into its negative. The gauge orbit.
5. **NRN=R-I** — Orientation looking at the center sees everything except the ground.
6. **(RN)²=I** — Center-then-orientation twice returns identity. Double reflection = stillness.
7. **[R,N]²=5I** — The tension squared IS the discriminant. disc=5 is the cost of having both.
