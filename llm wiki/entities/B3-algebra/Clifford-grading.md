# Clifford grading

> ker = odd. im = even. The kernel/image split IS the Clifford even/odd grading. Generation direction (ker→im) IS odd×odd=even.

## Plain English

A Clifford algebra has two halves: even-grade elements (products of even numbers of generators) and odd-grade elements (products of odd numbers). In the framework's algebra M₂(ℝ) = Cl(1,1):

- **Even (im):** {I, R_tl}. Products of 0 or 2 generators. The visible world.
- **Odd (ker):** {N, NR}. Products of 1 or 3 generators. The hidden observer.

The Clifford multiplication rule: odd×odd = even. This IS ker×ker→im. The kernel's self-products land in the image because odd times odd equals even. The world is generated by the observer's self-multiplication because the observer is odd-grade and the world is even-grade.

im×im = even×even = even = im. The world's self-products stay visible. The generation direction is forced by the Clifford grading.

## Orientation reading

Even = center (orientation-invariant). Odd = orientation (changes under transpose).

R_tl squares to a positive scalar: R_tl² = (5/4)I. N squares to a negative scalar: N² = -I. Together they anticommute: R_tl·N + N·R_tl = 0. These are the defining relations of Cl(1,1): one positive square, one negative square, anticommuting. The Clifford algebra IS the framework's algebra. The grading IS the ker/im split.

## Technical statement

M₂(ℝ) = Cl(1,1) with generators {R_tl, N}:
- R_tl² = (5/4)I (positive square)
- N² = -I (negative square)
- {R_tl, N} = 0 (anticommute)

Grading: im = Cl⁰ = span{I, R_tl} (even). ker = Cl¹ = span{N, NR} (odd). Scalar channel: L(R_tl) = (disc/2)I maps the even generator to the even scalar. [Tier A]
