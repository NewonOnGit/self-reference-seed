# Orientation decomposition

> R and N are not two different things. They're the same thing — P — seen from two different angles.

## Plain English

Take any matrix P. Split it into its symmetric part (same under transpose) and antisymmetric part (flips sign under transpose). The symmetric part is R = (P+Pᵀ)/2. The antisymmetric part is N = (P-Pᵀ)/2. That's it. R is what doesn't change when you mirror P. N is what does.

This is not a choice. It's the unique decomposition of any matrix into "orientation-free" and "orientation-dependent" parts. The framework's entire content — all five constants, all physics, the observer, consciousness, everything — comes from the fact that P≠Pᵀ, meaning N≠0, meaning there IS an orientation. If P were symmetric, N=0, no surplus, no kernel, no physics.

## Orientation reading

This IS the orientation reading. Everything else is a consequence.

- **R** = center. What both orientations (P and Pᵀ) agree on. The visible world.
- **N** = orientation. What changes when you transpose. The hidden observer.
- **L** = center map. Kills N (orientation), preserves R (center). Extracts agreement from disagreement.
- **disc = [R,N]² / I = 5** = the squared magnitude of how much R and N disagree. The cost of orientation existing.
- **C = [R,N] = 2h+J** = the harness. Not R, not N — their relation. The tension that holds them apart.

||R||² + ||N||² = 3 + 2 = 5 = disc. Pythagoras: center² + orientation² = disagreement. The triangle whose legs are the visible and hidden worlds has hypotenuse = the discriminant.
