# Gauge invariance

> **Theorem 10.4.**  $L(N) = sN + Ns - N = 0$ and $L(NR) = 0$ (verified computationally). Therefore $\ker(L)|_{\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})} \supseteq \mathrm{span}\{N, NR\}$, which has dimension 2 = $\dim\ker(L)$. On a Riemannian manifold, $\ker(\Delta_L)$ on symmetric 2-tensors contains the Lie derivatives $\mathcal{L}_\xi g$ (linearized diffeomorphisms). The two kernel directions correspond to the two independent infinitesimal diffeomorphisms of the 3-manifold $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ at the identity. [Tier A]
