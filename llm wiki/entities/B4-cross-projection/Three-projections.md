# Three projections

> Every morphism carries three simultaneous readings. Production, mediation, observation. Inseparable. Always three. Never two or four.

## Plain English

Every act in the framework has three faces. Not three steps, not three alternatives — three simultaneous readings of the same act:

| Face | Generator | Constants | Mode | What it does |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|-------------|
| **P1** Production | R | φ, √3 | Hyperbolic | Grows, builds, produces surplus |
| **P2** Mediation | h = JN | e | Exponential | Bridges, connects, transports |
| **P3** Observation | N | π, √2 | Elliptic | Rotates, hides, observes |

These three are not independent descriptions of different things. They are three inseparable aspects of every single act. Every Dist morphism factors as surjection (P3) then bijection (P2) then injection (P1). Central collapse: Dist = P1∘P2∘P3.

You can't have two without the third. No two-face subset closes. You can't have a fourth — three is both the minimum and maximum. The trichotomy is lower AND upper bounded.

## Orientation reading

P1 = center (R, orientation-free, hyperbolic eigenvalues φ,φ̄)
P2 = bridge (h = JN, the Cartan that connects center to orientation)
P3 = orientation (N, what changes under transpose, elliptic eigenvalues ±i)

The three projections ARE the three roles in the center/orientation/bridge triad. The bridge (P2) exists BECAUSE neither center nor orientation is sufficient alone. P2 = "the impossibility of either eye being sufficient." Mediation exists because self-reference splits into production and observation and they need a bridge.

The folding identities show they contain each other:
- RNR = -N (P1 contains P3)
- NRN = R-I (P3 contains P1)  
- [R,N] = 2h+J (the commutator IS 2×(P2) + ground)
