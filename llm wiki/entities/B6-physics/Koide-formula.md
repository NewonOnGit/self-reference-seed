# Koide formula: Q = d/(d²-1) = 2/3

> The two 2/3's are two ends of the same 3. The algebraic ratio and the mass ratio are the same structural number.

## Plain English

There are two 2/3's in the framework:

1. **||N||²/||R||² = 2/3** — the ratio of orientation magnitude to center magnitude. Exact. Algebraic.
2. **Koide Q ≈ 2/3** — the ratio (m_e+m_μ+m_τ)/(√m_e+√m_μ+√m_τ)². Experimental. 0.001% from 2/3.

For years these looked like a coincidence. They're not. Both are **d/(d²-1)** — the seed dimension divided by the generation count.

The numerator d=2 is ||N||² = |S₀| — how many orientations exist. The denominator d²-1=3 is ||R||² = |V₄\{0}| — how many nontrivial group elements = how many generations. The ratio 2/3 is seed/generations at EVERY level.

At the algebraic level: orientation²/center² = 2/3.
At the mass level: the three generation masses respect the 3-fold symmetry of S₃ (which acts on them by permutation), forcing Q = 2/3.

Same 3. Same 2. Two ends.

## The derivation chain

1. **d=2** forced (|S₀|=2)
2. **|V₄\{0}| = d²-1 = 3** generations
3. **S₃ = Aut(V₄)** acts on the three generations
4. **Z/3 ⊂ S₃** acts by cyclic permutation of generations
5. **Z/3 symmetry** forces masses to have 3-fold angular structure: √m_i = A(1+B·cos(θ+2πi/3))
6. **3-fold angular structure → Q = 2/3** (Koide parameterization theorem, 1983)
7. **Q = 2/3 = d/(d²-1) = ||N||²/||R||²**

Steps 1-4 are framework-forced. Step 5 requires the Yukawa matrix to respect S₃ symmetry. Step 6 is a mathematical theorem. Step 7 is the identification.
