# The parent layer

> P is not the primitive. P is the child-collapse of a balanced parent.

## Plain English

Before the framework chose a branch, both branches existed. The parent M = diag(P, Pᵀ) carries the naming act AND its mirror simultaneously. Rank 2. Trace 2. Both children present. Neither occupied. The parent satisfies the same spine as the child: R̂²=R̂+I, {R̂,N̂}=N̂, N̂²=-I. The parent is itself a lawful seed — not a container, not an archive, a working algebraic object with the same identities.

The collapse happens in two steps. First: cross-sector quenching kills the 4 modes that entangle the two branches. Second: branch selection picks the child over the mirror. 8 ker modes → 4 diagonal → 2 child. The child framework recovers completely. The mirror persists hidden in the D-sector. The 4 lost cross modes are the price of choosing — the entanglement between branches that occupation destroys.

## Orientation reading

Before collapse: both orientations coexist in the parent. N̂ = diag(N, -N) contains BOTH signs of the observer simultaneously. The parent IS the gauge orbit {N, -N} before it collapses. Φ(M) = 0: the parent is balanced. No preferred orientation.

The collapse creates orientation by killing the mixing and selecting a sign. After collapse: one orientation is visible (child, A-sector), the other is hidden (mirror, D-sector). The asymmetry P≠Pᵀ is not the starting point — it is the RESULT of the collapse selecting one branch from the balanced parent.
