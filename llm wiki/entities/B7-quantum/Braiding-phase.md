# Braiding phase

> e^(4πi/5). The discriminant divides the circle into 5 parts. Braiding statistics ARE disc acting through N.

## Plain English

When two Fibonacci anyons braid around each other, the quantum state picks up a phase of e^(4πi/5) — a rotation by 144 degrees. Why?

disc = 5 divides the circle into 5 equal parts (each 72°). A full braid traverses two parts: 2×72° = 144° = 4π/5 radians. cos(4π/5) = -φ/2. The braiding angle IS the discriminant acting as the natural partition of the circle through N-rotation.

## Orientation reading

Braiding = parallel transport via A=N. The disc-fold (circle ÷ 5) is the natural angular scale of orientation. exp(4π/5 · N) gives cos = -φ/2. The five constants, the discriminant, and braiding statistics are all one structure at different levels.
