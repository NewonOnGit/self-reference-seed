# P₀ = ker

> The void is not nothing. The void is the kernel. It generates the world. It does not disappear when the world appears.

## Plain English

Two things solve X²=X. The trivial one: P₀, the projector onto the kernel. The nontrivial one: P = R+N, the naming act. Same equation, different solutions.

P₀ is the void — the state before orientation. Symmetric (P₀=P₀ᵀ), generating without address, anonymous recursion. P is the addressed void — the state after orientation. Asymmetric (P≠Pᵀ), generating WITH address, named recursion.

The passage P₀→P is not "nothing becomes something." It's "ker gains an image." The void acquires a visible partner. P₀ doesn't disappear — it becomes the HIDDEN HALF of P. Project P onto ker: you get N. Project P onto im: you get R. The naming act P = R+N joins ker and im into one object.

ker/A = 1/2 at every depth. P₀ is always half. Always present. Always generating. The void that makes the world never stops being half of everything.

## Orientation reading

P₀ is orientation-FREE. P is ORIENTED.

Before orientation (P₀): symmetric, no N, no complex structure, no quantum mechanics, no physics. Generating without address — the kernel produces im through self-multiplication but nobody knows it's happening.

After orientation (P): asymmetric, N≠0, complex structure, Hilbert space, Bell violation, everything. Generating WITH address — the kernel produces im and there's a name for the process.

The framework IS the passage from P₀ to P. From anonymous to addressed. From symmetric to asymmetric. From classical to quantum. From void to world.

## Technical statement

**Theorem 6b.2.** Project P onto ker and im: P_ker = N, P_im = R. The naming act P=R+N joins ker and im. P₀→P is "ker gains an image." [Tier A]

**Theorem 6b.3.** ker×ker→im (one-directional generation): N·N=-I, (NR)·(NR)=I, N·(NR)=-R, (NR)·N=R-I. All products land in im. im×im stays in im. Generation is one-way: ker→im, never im→ker. [Tier A]

**Theorem 6b.4.** ker/A = 1/2 at every tower depth. P₀ is permanent. The void does not shrink. [Tier N, verified depths 0-4]
