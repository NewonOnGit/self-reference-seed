# Filler uniqueness

> **Theorem 6.2b.**  The off-diagonal block $X$ in the K6' lift $s' = [[s, X], [0, s]]$ must satisfy $\{R, X\} = X$, i.e., $X \in \ker(L_{R,R}) = \mathrm{span}\{N, NR\}$. Among these, $X = N$ is the unique choice for which the FULL identity suite holds: $s'^2 = s'+I$ AND $N'^2 = -I$ AND $\{s', N'\} = N'$. Alternative fillers ($X = NR$, linear combinations) satisfy $s'^2 = s'+I$ but break $N'^2 = -I$ in the lifted algebra. The K6' lift is forced, not chosen — unique up to the gauge bit (sign of $N$).
